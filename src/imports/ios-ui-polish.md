Here are the 3 subtle changes that will make this feel like a polished iOS product, not just a responsive web screen.

⸻

1. Turn the Photo Section into a “Card Group”

Right now the sections feel slightly separated instead of grouped.

Native iOS forms typically group related elements into cards.

Current mental structure

Fotos del juego
Añadir fotos
[grid]

Better structure

[ CARD ]

Fotos del juego                     4/10 · 40%
Mínimo 2 fotos · Máximo 10

+ Añadir fotos

[photo grid]

Visual rules:
	•	16px rounded card
	•	subtle background (secondarySystemBackground)
	•	16–20px padding

Why this works:
	•	reduces visual fragmentation
	•	feels much more native
	•	gives the upload flow a clear container

⸻

2. Replace the Progress Bar with a Step Header

Right now you have:

Paso 1 de 2
──────────────

But iOS apps typically use text hierarchy instead of bars for short flows.

Recommended

Paso 1 de 2
Información básica

Or even better:

Crear juego
Información básica

And remove the bar entirely.

Benefits:
	•	less visual noise
	•	clearer context
	•	more native to iOS patterns

Examples using this pattern:
	•	Airbnb listing creation
	•	Shopify product creation
	•	Apple onboarding flows

⸻

Bonus Micro-Improvement (very iOS)

Add a photo counter animation when adding images.

When a user uploads:

4/10 → 5/10

Use:
	•	small number transition
	•	subtle haptic feedback

Tiny detail, but makes the UI feel premium.
